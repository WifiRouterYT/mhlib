MORE ABOUT READING COUNTS

READING COUNTS - WHO, WHAT, WHY, WHERE, WHEN & HOW


Who can take a Reading Counts quiz?
  • Any student at Mission Hill

    • When do you take a RC quiz?
  • The Reading Counts system is active between 8:00 am and 3:30 pm, Monday - Friday.
  • You should take a quiz as soon as you finish a book.

    • Where do I take an RC quiz?
  • RC quizzes are taken on a laptop or computer at Mission Hill.
  • Reading Counts is not available off-campus.
  • You can use a Chromebook in a class during READ period, ELA period, or any other period if you are a Fast Finisher (a student who finishes an assignment early in class).

    • Why do I take an RC quiz?
  • RC quizzes are taken to track your success, reading progress, and how many words you read in one school year.
  • Your ELA teacher requires you to complete a book and take a Reading Counts quiz every 4-6 weeks for an assessment grade.

    • How to find a book with a Reading Counts quiz?
  • There are quizzes for over 48,000 fiction & non-fiction titles in the Reading Counts (RC) software. To find a Reading Counts quiz book at the Mission Hill Library look for the blue dots on the spine of each book which indicates they are a part of the RC program.
  • To find out if your book has a quiz select this link: Reading Counts Quick Find

    • How do I take an RC quiz?
  • Your USER NAME is your 5 digit student ID. It usually starts with a 6, 7, 8, or 9. It is the same number you use for lunch and checking out books from the library.
  • Your PASSWORD is the same as your user name, your 5 digit student ID.
  • Once you are logged in you can search for a Reading Counts quiz, take a Reading Counts quiz, and Check Your Progress to see how many words you have read so far.

    • What is a passing score, and what do I do if I don't pass the first time?
  • Each quiz has 10 multiple-choice questions. To pass you need to score 7/10, 8/10, 9/10, or 10/10. (Make sure you tell your ELA teacher your score!)
  • If you receive a 6/10 or lower: Review your wrong answers and print them if you can, take 24 hours to review and reread parts of the book you struggled to remember, try again, this time using the book to help you, and tell your ELA teacher you are working on your make-up.

    • CHECK IF YOUR BOOK HAS AN RC QUIZ!

    Look for a blue sticker on the spine of books! If it has a blue sticker/dot, it has a Reading Counts quiz.
